# Legitimate Interests Assessment (LIA)

**Data Controller:** Adaptly (operated by the user)

**Date of Assessment:** 08 March 2026

**Assessor:** Manus AI

---

## Part 1: The Purpose Test

> Does the processing relate to a legitimate interest? An interest can be commercial, individual, or societal. It must be real and of a genuine nature.

| Interest | Description | Is it Legitimate? |
|---|---|---|
| **Core Service Provision** | To provide the Adaptly platform to schools, including generating educational resources, managing pupil data, and facilitating communication between staff. This is our core commercial interest. | **Yes** |
| **System Security & Monitoring** | To protect the platform from unauthorised access, data breaches, and misuse. This includes logging IP addresses, user agent strings, and user actions for security auditing and incident response. | **Yes** |
| **Service Improvement** | To analyse aggregated, anonymised usage data (e.g., feature popularity, common errors) to identify areas for improvement, fix bugs, and enhance the user experience. | **Yes** |
| **Safeguarding** | To identify and flag potential safeguarding concerns based on content generated within the platform, in line with our legal and ethical obligations to protect children. | **Yes** |

**Conclusion:** All identified processing purposes are legitimate interests directly related to the provision and secure operation of an EdTech SaaS platform.

---

## Part 2: The Necessity Test

> Is the processing necessary to achieve that interest? "Necessary" means that the processing is a targeted and proportionate way of achieving the purpose. It does not mean it must be absolutely essential.

| Interest | Is Processing Necessary? | Justification |
|---|---|---|
| **Core Service Provision** | **Yes** | It is impossible to provide a personalised educational platform without processing personal data such as user accounts (teachers, staff), school information, and pupil data (initials, SEND status). |
| **System Security & Monitoring** | **Yes** | Logging is a fundamental and standard security practice. Without it, we could not detect or investigate security incidents, making the platform vulnerable and non-compliant with GDPR Article 32 (Security of Processing). |
| **Service Improvement** | **Yes** | While not strictly essential for the service to function, analysing usage patterns is the only effective way to make informed decisions about product development. The alternative (e.g., user surveys) is less reliable and more intrusive. All data is anonymised. |
| **Safeguarding** | **Yes** | Automated keyword filtering is a necessary and proportionate measure to meet our duty of care. It is not possible to manually review all content, and this automated approach is targeted at high-risk terms only. |

**Conclusion:** The processing activities are necessary for achieving the identified legitimate interests. There are no less intrusive, practical alternatives.

---

## Part 3: The Balancing Test

> Is the legitimate interest overridden by the individual’s interests, rights, or freedoms? We must balance our interests against those of the data subjects (teachers, pupils).

| Factor | Assessment & Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **Nature of the Data** | Includes special category data (pupil SEND status) and data relating to children. This requires a higher level of protection. **Mitigation:** We implement strict access controls (RBAC), data minimisation (initials only), and require schools (as Data Controllers) to confirm they have parental consent. |
| **Reasonable Expectations** | Users (school staff) and pupils would reasonably expect their data to be processed to provide the service. They would not expect it to be sold or used for unrelated marketing. **Mitigation:** Our Privacy Notice is clear and transparent. We only use data for the stated purposes. |
| **Impact of Processing** | The potential impact of a data breach is high, especially concerning safeguarding and SEND data. **Mitigation:** We employ strong security measures (encryption, access controls, audit logs) and have a clear data breach response plan. The new breach log will enhance this. |
| **Control over Data** | Users have a degree of control. They can request data erasure and portability. **Mitigation:** We provide GDPR-compliant endpoints for data subject rights and commit to a 30-day response time. |

### Provisional Outcome

The balance favours our legitimate interests.

### Final Decision

After implementing the following safeguards, the processing is justified on the basis of legitimate interests:

1.  **Strict Data Minimisation:** Continue to enforce the use of pupil initials only.
2.  **Robust Security:** Maintain and regularly review security measures.
3.  **Transparency:** Ensure the Privacy Notice remains up-to-date and easy to understand.
4.  **User Rights:** Uphold all data subject rights promptly.
5.  **ICO Registration:** Complete the mandatory ICO registration.

**Final Outcome:** We can rely on legitimate interests for the processing activities described in this assessment.

**Review Date:** This LIA should be reviewed annually or in the event of any significant change to the processing activities.
